The First Riot

One of the earliest known riots was the assasination of Roman dictator Gaius Julius Ceasar. Festering tensions between the Senate and Julius lead to his untimely death, in which he was assasinated by the order of multiple Senators. He was killed due to the fears of the Senate that Julius was taking away their power.

Born August 24, 1945, Marsha P. Johnson, named Malcolm Michales, was as self-identified drag queen and African American gay rights liberation activist.
A participant in the riots of 1969 for the rights of the LGBTQ community, Marsha P Johnson was one of the first protestors to fight back against the police raid of the Stonewall Inn, a safe haven for the LGBTQ community in an anti-queer society.
She passed under mysterious circumstances and was found on July 6, 1992, six days after reported missing after attending the 1992 Pride March. Her brave efforts for the movement of LGBTQ rights has put her name in the books as an activist and a role model.

✖

Borny July 18, 1918, Nelson Mandela made history as the president of South America from 1994-1999.
His government focused greatly on the dismantling of artheid in his country by adressing institutionalized racism.
The recipient of a Nobel Peace Prize, along with copious notable honors, Nelson Mandela is fondly remembered as the "Father of the Nation". He passed due to respiratory infection on December 5, 2013. His determination for racial equality is still remembered and his legacy lives on in the hearts of South Africa.

✖

Rosa L. M. Parks was born Febraury 4, 1913. Named the "first lady of civil rights" and "mother of the freedom movement".
In the face of segregation, Rosa Parks bravely defied the bus driver's order to give up her seat in the "colored section" of the bus to a white passenger after the "white section" was filled already. For her refusal to move, she was subsequently arrested. Her bravery, along with the participation in the Montgomery Bus Boycott pushed her into the limelight as an icon of the Civil Rights Movement.
At the age of 92, Rosa Parks peacefully passed away in her apartment, on October 24, 2005. After a lifetime of activism, Rosa Park's name will forever echo in the conversation of Black Rights, Anti-segregation Movements, and Civil Rights.
